## Fit check

- IUI describes itself as a venue at the intersection of Artificial Intelligence and Human-Computer Interaction.
- Official CFP topics that match this paper: intelligent user interfaces for generative AI; privacy and security of IUI; human control in daily automations; multimodal AI assistants; end-user interaction with LLMs, agents, and multimodal models; user control and steering of LLMs and agents; reproducibility including benchmarks, datasets, and challenges.
- Evidence bar: contributions are expected to be supported by rigorous evidence appropriate to claims, including system evaluation or computational analysis. This pack therefore cannot claim measured leakage reduction, false-block behavior, latency overhead, or task-completion impact until `eval/results/` exists.

## Format and submission

- Submission platform: Precision Conference Submission portal, SIGCHI → IUI 2027 → IUI 2027 Papers.
- Review manuscript format: single-column ACM manuscript using the latest ACM templates.
- LaTeX command required for review: `\documentclass[manuscript,review,anonymous]{acmart}`.
- Length policy: variable length proportional to contribution; authors are encouraged to stay within 10,000 words, and manuscripts over 12,000 words need an explanatory note.
- Originality: submissions must be original and not under consideration elsewhere.

## Double-blind and anonymity constraints

- Review is double-blind.
- The paper, video figures, and supplemental materials must omit author names and affiliations.
- Acknowledgements must be removed or anonymized during review.
- Self-citations must be written in the third person.
- Failure to follow anonymization guidelines may result in desk rejection.
- For this project: no public repository, public demo, author identity, organization identity, real user data, non-synthetic screen captures, secrets, production telemetry, or identifying URLs in review materials.

## GenAI disclosure requirement

- Submissions must comply with ACM policy on usage of GenAI.
- Text generated from large-scale language models must be clearly marked when used beyond editing the authors' own text.
- A `GenAI Usage Disclosure` section should appear right before references and disclose use of GenAI tools across research, code/data, and writing.
- The GenAI disclosure section and references do not count toward the word limit.

## Ethics and human-participants handling

- Research involving human subjects must follow the relevant ethics review requirements for the authors' research environment.
- Authors are asked to submit a short note to reviewers with that ethics-review context.
- Current scope is synthetic-only. Any human-subject user study remains blocked until an ethics path is cleared.
- Even for synthetic-only evaluation, include an ethics note explaining data provenance, synthetic fixture design, privacy-risk handling, and why no real participant data is used.

## Accessibility and supplemental materials

- Paper submissions should be accessible to reviewers.
- Videos, if submitted, need captions and should follow SIGCHI video guidelines.
- Supplemental material is optional but encouraged; any supplement must be anonymized.
- If there are closely related concurrent submissions based on the same study, artifact, or dataset, an anonymized version must be provided in the concurrent-submissions field.

## Re-check triggers

Re-check the official CFP before any of the following: paper skeleton creation, final abstract freeze, supplement packaging, submission, or any claim about deadlines, formatting, anonymization, ethics, GenAI disclosure, accessibility, open access, or required artifacts.
